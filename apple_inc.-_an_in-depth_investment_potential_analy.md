The company continued to innovate, and in 2001, it introduced the iPod, a product that revolutionized the way people listened to music by allowing them to carry thousands of songs in their pockets. The iPod was a critical success and propelled Apple into the consumer electronics space, laying the groundwork for future innovations. The subsequent release of iTunes further expanded Apple's ecosystem, changing the music industry's distribution model.

Perhaps the most transformative product in Apple's history was the introduction of the iPhone in 2007. The iPhone combined a mobile phone, an iPod, and an internet communicator in a single device, effectively creating the modern smartphone market. It was not only a commercial success but also a catalyst for the development of an entire ecosystem of applications through the App Store, launched in 2008 [Source: AAPL_profile]. The iPhone remains Apple's largest revenue generator, accounting for a significant portion of its annual sales, demonstrating its enduring impact on the company's financial success.

In 2015, Apple ventured into wearable technology with the launch of the Apple Watch. This product introduced consumers to a new way of interacting with technology, focusing on health and fitness monitoring, and further solidifying Apple's presence in the consumer electronics market. The Apple Watch has since evolved into a leading product in the wearables sector, contributing to Apple's diversification of revenue streams [Source: AAPL_profile].

Throughout its history, Apple has consistently demonstrated an ability to innovate and redefine markets.
